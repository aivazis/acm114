Objectives

This course is an introduction to concurrent algorithms and programming techniques with a focus on scientific applications. I will try to pay equal attention to the implications of concurrency on algorithms, numerical techniques and software engineering.

I will try to make as few assumptions about your background as possible. I will present an overview of the basic ideas in algorithm design and analysis, discuss relevant data structures as the need arises, and walk through short introductions to all the numerical techniques we will be investigating. However, I am assuming that you are competent in some programming language, with a strong preference for C or C++, although I will try to accommodate FORTRAN programmers. Some of the later material will require a superficial knowledge of Python, an interpreted language that is becoming a common fixture in high performance computing.

We will be making extensive use of MPI, the de facto standard environment for scientific computing, and the only widely available solution for writing parallel programs that scale to thousands of processors. We will cover both basic and advanced techniques, including some of the improvements from version 2.0 of the standard. You should expect to be a comfortable, and perhaps sophisticated, MPI user by the end of the term.
